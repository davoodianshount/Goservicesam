Frequently asked questions

How do I know if I have a Zinsco or Federal Pacific panel?
Open your panel cover. The manufacturer name is printed on the inside of the door or on a label near the main breaker. Zinsco and Federal Pacific (also labeled FPE or Stab-Lok) panels were installed heavily between about 1950 and 1980 in Southern California. If you're not sure, send us a photo of the inside of the panel door and we'll tell you within 24 hours — free.
How long does a panel upgrade take?
Most 100A → 200A residential upgrades are completed in one business day. The power is off for 4–6 hours during the swap. If the meter base or service drop also needs updating, the utility coordination can add a day to the schedule.
Do I need a permit?
Yes. We pull the permit through the city building department (LADBS, Glendale, Burbank, etc.) and handle the final inspection. The permit is included in your quote — no add-on surprises.
What if I'm not sure my panel needs upgrading?
Call us for a free on-site evaluation. We will check the panel label, look at the service size printed on the main breaker, inspect the condition of the breakers, and give you an honest assessment — often the answer is 'you're fine for now' and we tell you that too.
